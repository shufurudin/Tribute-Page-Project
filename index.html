Asashoryu

The Yokozuna

Asashoryu Akinori is his rikishi name and he is natural from Mongolia. He has a strong background on the fight scene, his father and their old brothers achieved high ranks in the mongolian wrestling sport, since young Asashoryu practiced this sport and Judo later.
He was one the most successful rikishi in the hole sumo history, being the first Mongolian to rise at the highest rank of sumo (Yokozuna) and the first rikishi to win all the 6 bashos in the year!
He won 25 top division tournament championships, putting him in the fourth place of all time in the sport.

But he is more like a anti-hero than a hero. Asashoryu was envolve in a lot of miss conducts, in the dohyo, for example, he was the first Yokozuna to receive a hansoku-make in a bout, out the dohyo unfortunately he made several bad actions from be seen without dressing the typical kimono until aggressions and drunking.

Asashoryu had a hard discipline and dedication on keiko, making several repetitions with dumbbells of 30kgs, lifting 200kgs on chest press and carring a big bowl with concrete during his suriashi.
His style of sumo is impressive, even being considered a light weight in sumo he was ever confident in their strong and speed, giving to him a wide pool of techniques. Technically speaking, his style of sumo is considered a migi-yotsu when gripped on the mawashi of his opponent and tsuppari when in ranged.
Due his impetuous fight spirit, very often threw the opponent with a peculiar trow. He mark 45 different kimarite during his carrer, a really big number in comparision with others rikishis with same level.
